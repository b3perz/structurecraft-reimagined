We didn't set out to build a category.
We just kept solving problems.

1980s

Gerald and Paul start as structural engineers doing residential work in the Fraser Valley. Mennonite values: frugality, helpfulness, practical common sense. Builders come up the back steps with dirty boots, looking for solutions.

Old photo: Gerald at a drafting table, paper drawings, early office

1994

Gerald travels to Europe. Sees Arup, Piano, Foster. Realizes engineering has separated from building in North America. Decides to put them back together — like Michelangelo, who designed, engineered, and built.

Scanned sketch: Gerald's notes from the Europe trip, pencil on hotel notepad

1998

StructureCraft founded. First major project: Vancouver Aquarium. First lesson: taking construction risk is terrifying and essential. The company name is deliberate — "Structure," not "Timber." Each material has its place.

Photo: Vancouver Aquarium timber structure under construction, early SC crew on site

2005

Richmond Olympic Oval. One of the first all-timber long-span structures in North America. Built with pine beetle-killed 2x4s — turning ecological disaster into architectural opportunity. Gerald: "That was the first project that put us on the map."

Interior: Olympic Oval, vast timber lattice ceiling spanning above the speed skating rink, natural light

2008

The recession nearly kills the company. Lucas returns from England. Writes the business plan. Secures funding. Builds the fabrication facility in Abbotsford. The hardest year becomes the foundation for everything after.

Photo: the Abbotsford fabrication facility in early days, concrete floor, first CNC router, morning light

2013–2020

T3 with Hines. Five buildings. The backbone of revenue for a decade. Proof that beauty and efficiency can be the same project, repeated and perfected. Lucas joins full-time in 2013 and stabilizes operations. The team grows to 100+.

Photo: T3 office building exterior, clean mass timber bays, urban streetscape, pedestrians walking

2024

DowelLam spins out as its own brand. The DLT product that SC pioneered now stands on its own, freeing StructureCraft to return to its core: engineers and builders first, manufacturers second. The separation removes the perception that SC is "just pushing their own product."

Photo: DLT panel being fabricated in the shop, CNC precision, dowels visible in cross-section

Now

27 years in. Still curious about your problem. Not our own solution.

"Common sense has to be. We're not looking to create conflict with architects, but I always tried to bring back some sense of 'this is practical.'"
— Gerald Epp, Founder
