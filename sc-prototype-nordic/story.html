We didn't set out to build a category.
We just kept asking questions.

1980s

Gerald and Paul start as structural engineers doing residential work in the Fraser Valley. Practical people. Builders would come up the back steps looking for solutions.

1994

Gerald travels to Europe. Sees firms where engineers and builders work closely together. Wonders why that's become so rare in North America.

1998

StructureCraft founded. First project: Vancouver Aquarium. First lesson: taking construction risk is uncomfortable, but it's where you learn the most. The name was deliberate — "Structure," not "Timber."

2005

Richmond Olympic Oval. A long-span roof built with pine beetle-killed lumber that nobody else wanted. It turned out to be both the most practical and the most interesting solution.

2008

The recession nearly closes the company. Lucas returns from England, writes a business plan, secures funding, builds the fabrication facility. A difficult year that turned out to be important.

2013–2020

T3 with Hines. Five buildings over a decade, each one a little more refined than the last. Lucas joins full-time. The team grows past 100.

2024

DowelLam spins out as its own company. StructureCraft returns to what it's always been: engineers and builders who pick the right material for each project.

Now

Twenty-seven years in. Still learning. Still not attached to any one answer.

"Practical doesn't mean boring — it means honest."
— Gerald Epp, Founder
